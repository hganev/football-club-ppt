1/2 KEY REVENUE STREAMS
Player Sales:
● Key plank of the business plan
● Develop and identify potential talents and then sell
them and so to tap into the massive and growing player
transfer market
● Achieve signiﬁcant amounts through good scouting and
player development over the years
New revenue streams:
● Develop speciﬁed content for global football fans
● Focusing on behind the scenes access. This will be
content that dedicated and passionate football fans
would like to see in terms of the behind the scenes
operations of a football club. Something that is currently
largely a mystery to most fans of the world’s most
popular sport
● This would attract general football fans interested in the
football business rather than club speciﬁc fans
● Provide access to:
○ Half time and post game coach speeches to the players
○ Match analysis preparation and presentations
○ Scouting analysis, detailed player diaries/documentaries
Player sales assumption:
● Sell 2 players at the end of each season
● Following second division season, players expected to be
sold for 50,000-100,000 euro
● Following ﬁrst division season, players expected to be sold
for 250,000-500,000 euro
● Once reputation for development established and regularly
playing in the ﬁrst division, expected average transfer value to
be at least 500,000 euro and to transfer 2-3 players per
season. So after two seasons in the ﬁrst division, it is
expected the club to be making 1-1.5 million euro from
transfer fees alone per season
Revenue streams assumption:
● Paywall of 2-3 euro per month
● First season in division 2 no subscribers (2nd season in
Division 2 - 5,000 ﬁrst half year, 10,000 second half year)
● First season in Division 1 - 5,000 subscribers ﬁrst half year,
15,000 subscribers second half year
● Second season in Division 1 - 15,000 subscribers ﬁrst half
year, 30,000 subscribers second half year
● Signiﬁcant growth each year that we are in Division 1 and
performing and developing talent and content
2/2 KEY REVENUE STREAMS
International focus:
● Sign players from emerging football countries that have big
football economies, but have had limited player exports and
virtually zero to Europe. Looking at players from Thailand,
Indonesia, India, Vietnam, Guatemala and the like. Players be
good enough to perform in Europe, but we would look to
build content around that player.
● Tailor sponsorship and advertising speciﬁc to key subscriber
countries. Advertisers would be diﬀerent for shows streamed
in Australia to the ones in the USA or the ones in Asia. Global
advertisers also available across all subscribers. Tailor
subscribers based on country of origin and thereby manage
the costs per country to better suit their standard of living.
● Private Academy Model linked with University Study -
common in Italy, Spain and Portugal and increasingly popular
in the UK, Netherlands and elsewhere. They are run by having
the players pay to attend and in return receive professional
full-time training + education (if desired).
● Bulgaria is well suited to this model - provide a cost
competitive oﬀering to those currently in the market. Could
be run at slightly lower costs than similar operations in
Southern Europe and signiﬁcantly cheaper than Northern
European models. This would allow for either greater
proﬁtability or a lower cost to attract potential players.

● Subscribers are as above and driven partly

● International sponsorship will be driven by

Division 1, estimate of 20,000 in second
